Customer Data Platform (CDP) - Technical Specifications

1. Executive Summary

This Requirements Specification Document (RSD) defines the technical and functional requirements for implementing a Customer Data Platform (CDP) to unify QuickFix's fragmented data systems. The CDP will eliminate data silos across Marketing, Billing, Support, and Backend Operations, enabling real-time customer analytics and data-driven decision making.

Project Scope & Objectives
  • Primary Goal: Integrate 4 disconnected systems into unified customer data architecture
  • Success Criteria: 80% reduction in data fragmentation, real-time customer profitability tracking
  • Timeline: 12-month phased implementation approach
  • Budget Impact: ROI positive within 18 months through operational efficiency gains

5.3 Security & Compliance

Data Protection Requirements
  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption for data at rest, TLS 1.3 for data in transit
  • Access Control: Role-based access control (RBAC) with multi-factor authentication
  • Audit Logging: Complete audit trail of all data access and modifications
  • Compliance: GDPR compliance for EU customers, SOC 2 Type II certification required
  • Data Retention: Configurable retention policies with automated data purging

8. Risk Assessment & Mitigation

Critical Risk Factors
  • Data Migration Risk: Potential data loss during system integration
    Mitigation: Comprehensive backup strategy, phased migration approach
  • System Downtime: Business disruption during implementation
    Mitigation: Blue-green deployment strategy, rollback procedures
  • User Adoption: Resistance to new workflows and interfaces
    Mitigation: Change management program, comprehensive training
  • Technical Complexity: Integration challenges with legacy systems
    Mitigation: Proof of concept development, experienced integration team
